I am currently a Visiting Faculty Researcher in the market algorithms group at Google Research NYC. I will be joining University of Chicago, Booth School of Business (Operations Management) as an Assistant Professor in July 2020.

I received my PhD from the Computer Science Department(minored in Applied Mathematics) at Cornell University under the supervision of Prof. Robert Kleinberg. After that, I was a Motwani Postdoctoral Researcher at Stanford University (Department of Computer Science) primarily mentored by Prof. Amin Saberi, and jointly by Prof. Tim Roughgarden and Prof. Moses Charikar.

I study the interplay between algorithms, incentives, and learning, with a focus on applications in revenue management and market design. Topics include:

  • Algorithmic mechanism design and game theory,

  • Online learning theory and applications,

  • Online, stochastic and non-convex optimization,

  • Algorithmic aspects of machine learning.
